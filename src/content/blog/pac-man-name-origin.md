Before Pac-Man became a global icon, he was known in Japan as "Puck Man" — a name drawn from the phrase "paku-paku," used to describe the sound and motion of a mouth opening and closing.

When Namco brought the game to the US, the name changed to Pac-Man. One widely told reason: arcade operators worried that "Puck Man" cabinets would get vandalized, with the P scratched into a ruder letter. Whether or not that was the deciding factor, "Pac-Man" stuck — and outlived just about every arcade trend that came after it.
