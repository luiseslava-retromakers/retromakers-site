GoldenEye 007's split-screen multiplayer wasn't part of the original plan — it was bolted on later in development, almost as an afterthought. It ended up becoming the mode most people associate with the game decades later.

The small Rare team behind it had relatively little shooter experience going in, which some developers have since pointed to as a reason the game felt willing to break from FPS conventions of the era rather than just copy them.
