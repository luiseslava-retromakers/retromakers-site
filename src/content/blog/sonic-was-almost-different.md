Sega needed a mascot that could go toe-to-toe with Mario, and Sonic the Hedgehog was built for exactly that job: fast, blue to match the Sega logo, and just cool enough to win over teenagers who'd outgrown Nintendo's plumber.

The final design beat out other early concepts, including character ideas that didn't make the cut for the mascot role at all. Speed wasn't just a gameplay gimmick, either — it doubled as a technical showcase, proving what the Genesis hardware could do that competing consoles couldn't quite match.
